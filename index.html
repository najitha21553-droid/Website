About

Dr. A. Najitha Banu is an Associate Professor in the Department of Zoology, School of Bioengineering and BioSciences at Lovely Professional University, Phagwara, Punjab. She earned her Ph.D. from Thiagarajar College, Madurai (affiliated to Madurai Kamaraj University) in 2015.

Her doctoral research focused on the biosynthesis and characterization of silver nanoparticles using bacterial and fungal entomopathogens against human vector mosquitoes — a field where she continues to be a prolific contributor.

A recipient of the prestigious Maulana Azad National Fellowship (2010–2014), she has guided 6 Ph.D. scholars to completion and has 41+ peer-reviewed publications with an h-index of 11 and over 666 citations.

Research Focus

🔬
Silver Nanoparticles Biosynthesis
Synthesizing eco-friendly silver nanoparticles using entomopathogenic bacteria and fungi as potent mosquito larvicidal agents against dengue and malaria vectors.
🦟
Medical Entomology & Vector Control
Investigating sustainable biological control strategies against human vector mosquitoes including Aedes aegypti and Culex quinquefasciatus using biopesticides.
🍄
Entomopathogenic Fungi
Evaluating fungal entomopathogens like Beauveria bassiana and Isaria fumosorosea for insect pest management and nanoparticle synthesis applications.
⚗️
Green Nanotechnology
Developing phytofabricated and myco-synthesized nanoparticles using plant extracts and microbes for antimicrobial and biopesticidal activities.
🌾
Agricultural Entomology
Studying population dynamics of agricultural pests, integrated pest management, and the impact of pesticides on non-target arthropod diversity.
💊
Drug Delivery & Nanomedicine
Exploring nanomaterial-based platforms for antiparasitic activity, metabolic disorder management, and novel drug delivery systems.
